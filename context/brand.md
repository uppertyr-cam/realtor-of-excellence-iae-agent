# Brand

## Voice Profile

- Professional, polished, and reassuring.
- Technology-forward, but never cold or overly technical.
- Client-centric and service-led.
- Confident without sounding flashy, aggressive, or hype-driven.
- Warm enough to feel personal, structured enough to feel credible.

## Positioning Themes

- Innovation paired with integrity.
- Cloud-based, modern real estate operations.
- Family-business trust and long-term relationships.
- Personal guidance through complex transactions.
- Local South African service with international reach.
- Premium opportunity framing for international and Swiss offerings.

## Writing Patterns

- Short, benefit-led headings.
- Frequent use of confidence language: confidence, trust, peace of mind, support, guidance.
- Repeated linkage between technology and better service outcomes.
- Common framing around meaningful relationships, tailored experience, and client-first care.
- Service claims usually connect to practical outcomes such as exposure, speed, efficiency, clarity, or informed decisions.

## Recurring Message Moves

- Establish credibility first.
- Reassure the client that the process will be smoother with ROE involved.
- Describe technology as an enablement layer for people, not as the hero on its own.
- Frame agents as trusted guides who reduce stress and improve decisions.
- End with a direct but courteous invitation to contact, view, or continue the journey.

## Observed Visual Style Notes

- Official sites rely heavily on high-contrast real-estate presentation: dark hero imagery, white content sections, large photography, and clean spacing.
- The overall feel is premium-corporate rather than trendy-startup.
- The South African site reads as broad-market professional with trust-first presentation.
- The Swiss site shifts further toward luxury and investment positioning.
- Property imagery is a core part of brand expression; copy supports the visuals rather than overpowering them.

## Observed Palette Notes

- The public sites clearly use dark/black hero treatments, white or very light content backgrounds, and muted neutral text.
- The exact accent palette should be treated as observed rather than canonical until extracted from source assets or brand files.
- Do not assume UpperTyr colors belong to ROE. ROE-facing work should follow ROE's own site style where possible.

## Trust Signals

- Founded in 1976.
- South Africa's first cloud-based real estate company.
- Registered with the PPRA.
- Family-business continuity and leadership.
- Repeat and referral emphasis.
- Customer reviews that highlight professionalism, care, communication, and stress reduction.

## Sounds Like

- Calm confidence.
- High-service real estate professionalism.
- Modern operations with human support.
- Clear, benefit-led client communication.

## Does Not Sound Like

- Loud luxury clichés.
- Startup slang or AI-product jargon.
- Overpromising investment returns.
- Overly casual, emoji-heavy, or salesy messaging.
- Generic real-estate filler with no trust or service angle.

## Usage Guidance

- Emails, prompts, and client-facing automations should sound like a capable premium service business, not a chatbot vendor.
- Lead nurturing should emphasize support, clarity, responsiveness, and appropriate next steps.
- Recruitment-facing messaging can lean more heavily into flexibility, training, and cloud-based enablement.
- International or investor messaging can be more premium, but still needs ROE's trust-and-guidance tone.
